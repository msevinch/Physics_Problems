
---

### 1. Identify the Physical Constants
* **Electron charge ($q$ or $e$):** $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Electron mass ($m$):** $9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Potential Difference ($V$):** $5000 \text{ V}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.1 \text{ T}$

---

### 2. Step 1: Determine the Velocity ($v$)
When an electron is accelerated through a potential difference, the electrical potential energy is converted into kinetic energy. According to the **Work-Energy Theorem**:

$$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Solving for velocity ($v$):

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C})(5000 \text{ V})}{9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}}}$$

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{1.602 \times 10^{-15}}{9.109 \times 10^{-31}}} \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### 3. Step 2: Determine the Radius ($r$)
Once the electron enters the magnetic field, it experiences a **magnetic force** (Lorentz force). Because the field is perpendicular to the velocity, this force acts as a **centripetal force**, causing the electron to move in a circle.



**Theoretical Equivalence:**

$$F_{\text{magnetic}} = F_{\text{centripetal}}$$

$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Rearranging the formula to solve for the radius ($r$):

$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

**Calculation:**

$$r = \frac{(9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg})(4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s})}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C})(0.1 \text{ T})}$$

$$r = \frac{3.816 \times 10^{-23}}{1.602 \times 10^{-20}}$$

$$r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$

---

### Final Result
The radius of the circular path followed by the electron is:
**$2.38 \text{ mm}$** (or $0.00238 \text{ m}$)

> **A Note on Precision:** At $5000 \text{ V}$, the electron is traveling at approximately **14% the speed of light**. While relativistic effects are starting to appear, they would only change the result by about **0.5%**, so the classical (non-relativistic) calculation used here is appropriate for most physics contexts.
