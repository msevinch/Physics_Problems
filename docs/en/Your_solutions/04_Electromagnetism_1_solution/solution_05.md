
---

### 1. Identify the Physical Constants
* **Mass of the proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Charge of the proton ($e$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Gravitational acceleration ($g$):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

### 2. Determine the Theory (Force Balance)
For the proton to remain stationary (levitate), the net force acting on it must be zero ($\sum F = 0$).

1.  **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** Acts downward.

    $$F_g = m_p \cdot g$$
    
3.  **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Acts in the direction of the electric field (since a proton is positively charged). To oppose gravity, this must act upward.

      $$F_e = e \cdot E$$

Setting the magnitudes equal to each other for equilibrium:

$$e \cdot E = m_p \cdot g$$



### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step A: Rearrange the formula to solve for $E$**

$$E = \frac{m_p \cdot g}{e}$$

**Step B: Substitute the values**

$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \times (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$

**Step C: Perform the multiplication in the numerator**

$$m_p \cdot g = 1.6366 \times 10^{-26} \text{ N}$$

**Step D: Divide by the charge**

$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}$$

$$E \approx 1.022875 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$

---

### Final Result
* **Electric Field Strength ($E$):** $\approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$ (or V/m)
* **Direction:** **Upward** (The field must point up so that the force on the positive proton is directed against gravity).

> **Contextual Note:** This is an incredibly small electric field strength. To put this in perspective, a standard AA battery can create a field of roughly $100 \text{ V/m}$ across a small gap. The reason such a tiny field can lift a proton is that the mass of a proton is extremely small compared to its charge-to-mass ratio.
