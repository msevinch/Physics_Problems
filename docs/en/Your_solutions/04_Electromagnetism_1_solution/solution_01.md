
### 1. Define the System Geometry
First, we need to find the distance between each corner charge ($q$) and the central charge ($Q$).
* **Square side ($s$):** $1.0\text{ m}$.
* **Diagonal of the square ($d$):** Using the Pythagorean theorem ($a^2 + b^2 = c^2$):
    $$d = \sqrt{1.0^2 + 1.0^2} = \sqrt{2}\text{ m} \approx 1.414\text{ m}$$
* **Distance from corner to center ($r$):** The center is exactly half the diagonal:
    $$r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\text{ m} \approx 0.707\text{ m}$$



---

### 2. Calculate Individual Force Magnitude
According to **Coulomb’s Law**, the magnitude of the electric force between two point charges is:
$$F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$

Where:
* $k \approx 8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$ (Coulomb's constant)
* $q_1 = 1.0\text{ C}$
* $q_2 = -2.0\text{ C}$
* $r^2 = (\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})^2 = 0.5\text{ m}^2$

Plugging in the numbers:
$$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{|(1.0)(-2.0)|}{0.5}$$
$$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \times 4 = 3.596 \times 10^{10}\text{ N}$$

Each corner charge exerts a force of approximately **$35.96\text{ GN}$** on the central charge. Since the corner charges are positive and the central charge is negative, these forces are **attractive** (directed from the center toward each corner).

---

### 3. Apply the Principle of Superposition
The net force $\vec{F}_{\text{net}}$ is the vector sum of all four individual forces:
$$\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \vec{F}_1 + \vec{F}_2 + \vec{F}_3 + \vec{F}_4$$

Let's look at the symmetry:
1.  **Opposite Corners (Pair A):** The charge at the top-left corner pulls the central charge toward the top-left. The charge at the bottom-right corner pulls the central charge toward the bottom-right with the **exact same magnitude**. These two vectors are equal and opposite, so they cancel out.
2.  **Opposite Corners (Pair B):** The charge at the top-right corner pulls the central charge toward the top-right. The charge at the bottom-left corner pulls the central charge toward the bottom-left. Again, these vectors are equal and opposite, so they cancel out.

---

### Final Result
* **Magnitude:** $0\text{ N}$
* **Direction:** None (The charge is in electrostatic equilibrium).

**The Bottom Line:** Because the four charges at the corners are identical and the square is perfectly symmetrical, the pulls from each corner perfectly balance each other out. No matter how large the charges are, the net force at the dead center remains zero.
