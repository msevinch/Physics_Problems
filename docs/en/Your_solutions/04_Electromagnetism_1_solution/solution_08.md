
---

### 1. Identify the Theory
The magnetic force $\vec{F}_B$ exerted on a charge $q$ moving with velocity $\vec{v}$ through a magnetic field $\vec{B}$ is given by the vector cross product:

$$\vec{F}_B = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$

The **magnitude** of this force is calculated as:

$$F = |q| v B \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
* $q$ is the charge of the particle.
* $v$ is the speed of the particle.
* $B$ is the magnetic field strength.
* $\theta$ is the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector.




---

### 2. Identify the Given Parameters
* **Charge ($q$):** $2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5 \text{ T}$
* **Speed ($v$):** $10^6 \text{ m/s}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (since the particle enters "perpendicular to the field").
* **Mass ($m$):** $4 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$ (Note: Mass is not required to calculate the *force*, only to find the resulting acceleration or path radius).

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step A: Determine the sine of the angle**
Since the motion is perpendicular:

$$\sin(90^\circ) = 1$$

**Step B: Plug the values into the magnitude formula**

$$F = (2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times (10^6 \text{ m/s}) \times (0.5 \text{ T}) \times 1$$

**Step C: Solve the arithmetic**
1.  Multiply the constants: $2 \times 0.5 = 1$
2.  Combine the powers of ten: $10^{-19} \times 10^6 = 10^{-13}$

$$F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$$

---

### Final Result
The magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle is:
**$1.0 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$**

> **Quick Insight:** You might notice we didn't use the mass ($m$) at all! In physics problems, sometimes "extra" information is provided to see if you can identify which physical laws apply. If you wanted to find the **acceleration** of the particle, you would then use Newton's Second Law ($a = F/m$), which would yield a massive $2.5 \times 10^{13} \text{ m/s}^2$.
