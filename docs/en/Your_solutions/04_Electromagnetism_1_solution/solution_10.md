
### 1. The Underlying Theory
The magnetic force ($\vec{F}$) on a straight wire of length $L$ carrying a current $I$ in a uniform magnetic field $\vec{B}$ is given by:

$$\vec{F} = I(\vec{L} \times \vec{B})$$

The **magnitude** of this force is calculated using the formula:

$$F = I \cdot L \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
* **$I$:** Current in Amperes (A)
* **$L$:** Length of the wire in meters (m)
* **$B$:** Magnetic field strength in Tesla (T)
* **$\theta$:** The angle between the direction of the current and the magnetic field lines.



---

### 2. Identify Given Values
* **$L = 2.0\text{ m}$**
* **$I = 10\text{ A}$**
* **$B = 0.5\text{ T}$**

First, let's calculate the product of the constants ($I \cdot L \cdot B$):

$$I \cdot L \cdot B = 10 \cdot 2.0 \cdot 0.5 = 10.0\text{ N}$$

This represents the **maximum possible force** (when the wire is perpendicular to the field).

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculations

#### **a) When the angle is $90^\circ$**
The wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field.
* **Theory:** $\sin(90^\circ) = 1.0$
* **Calculation:** $F = 10.0 \times 1.0$
* **Result:** **$10.0\text{ N}$**

#### **b) When the angle is $45^\circ$**
The wire is at a diagonal to the field.
* **Theory:** $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.7071$
* **Calculation:** $F = 10.0 \times 0.7071$
* **Result:** **$7.07\text{ N}$**

#### **c) When the angle is $0^\circ$**
The wire is parallel to the magnetic field.
* **Theory:** $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$
* **Calculation:** $F = 10.0 \times 0$
* **Result:** **$0\text{ N}$**

---

### Summary Table

| Angle ($\theta$) | Orientation | $\sin(\theta)$ | Force ($F$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$90^\circ$** | Perpendicular | $1.0$ | **$10.0\text{ N}$** |
| **$45^\circ$** | Diagonal | $\approx 0.707$ | **$7.07\text{ N}$** |
| **$0^\circ$** | Parallel | $0$ | **$0\text{ N}$** |

**Crucial Insight:** The force is strongest when the wire "cuts" across the magnetic field lines at a right angle. If the wire runs perfectly alongside the field lines (parallel), no magnetic force is generated at all, regardless of how much current is flowing!
