## 5. Trigonometry

This document provides a step-by-step calculation for the horizontal and vertical components of a vector in a 2D coordinate system.

---

### 1. Theory and Formulas

**Vector Decomposition** is the process of breaking a single vector into two or more vectors that, when added together, equal the original vector. In a 2D Cartesian plane, a vector $\vec{A}$ is typically broken down into its horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) components.



#### Fundamental Formulas
Given a vector with magnitude $A$ and an angle $\theta$ relative to the positive x-axis:

* **Horizontal Component ($A_x$):** $A_x = A \cos(\theta)$
* **Vertical Component ($A_y$):** $A_y = A \sin(\theta)$

These formulas are derived from the definitions of sine and cosine in a right-angled triangle, where the vector $\vec{A}$ acts as the hypotenuse.



---

### 2. Step-by-Step Determination

**Given Data:**
* Magnitude ($A$) = $15$
* Angle ($\theta$) = $60^\circ$

#### Step 1: Calculate the Horizontal Component ($A_x$)
Using the cosine function:
$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$
Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = \mathbf{7.5}$$

#### Step 2: Calculate the Vertical Component ($A_y$)
Using the sine function:
$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:
$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx \mathbf{12.99}$$
