
### **Part 1: Theory**

When capacitors are connected in a parallel circuit, there are three fundamental rules that govern their behavior:

1. **Voltage:** The potential difference (voltage, $V$) across each individual capacitor in parallel is exactly the same as the voltage of the source.

$$V_{total} = V_1 = V_2$$


2. **Equivalent Capacitance:** The total (or equivalent) capacitance $C_{eq}$ of the circuit is simply the sum of the individual capacitances. Connecting them in parallel effectively increases the total plate area, allowing them to store more charge.

$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2$$


3. **Charge and Energy:** The total charge $Q_{total}$ is the sum of the charges on each capacitor ($Q = C \times V$). The total stored potential energy $U_{total}$ can be calculated using the equivalent capacitance and the total voltage using the standard energy formula:

$$U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2$$

### **Part 2: Determine**

**1. Identify the Given Variables**

* Capacitor 1 ($C_1$) = $4\,\mu\text{F} = 4 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}$
* Capacitor 2 ($C_2$) = $6\,\mu\text{F} = 6 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}$
* Voltage ($V$) = $10\text{ V}$

**2. Determine the Equivalent Capacitance ($C_{eq}$)**
Using the parallel capacitance rule:


$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2$$

$$C_{eq} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F}$$

$$C_{eq} = 10\,\mu\text{F} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}$$

**3. Determine the Total Charge ($Q_{total}$)**
Using the formula for charge ($Q = C \times V$) with our total equivalent capacitance:


$$Q_{total} = C_{eq} \times V$$

$$Q_{total} = (10 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}) \times 10\text{ V}$$

$$Q_{total} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}$$


**Total Charge = $100\,\mu\text{C}$** *(Note: Alternatively, you could find the charge on $C_1$ as $40\,\mu\text{C}$ and $C_2$ as $60\,\mu\text{C}$, and add them together to get the same $100\,\mu\text{C}$).*

**4. Determine the Total Stored Energy ($U_{total}$)**
Using the energy formula with our equivalent capacitance:


$$U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2$$

$$U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}) \times (10\text{ V})^2$$

$$U_{total} = (5 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}) \times 100\text{ V}^2$$

$$U_{total} = 500 \times 10^{-6}\text{ J}$$

$$U_{total} = 5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ J}$$


**Total Energy = $0.5\text{ mJ}$** (millijoules) or $5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ J}$
