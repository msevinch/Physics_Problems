### 4. Energy & Momentum


### Fundamental Theory

This problem must be solved in two distinct stages because different physical conservation laws apply to each stage:

1.  **Stage 1: The Slide (Conservation of Mechanical Energy)**
    As the first block slides down the frictionless track, Earth's gravity does work on it. Its initial Gravitational Potential Energy ($PE$) is entirely converted into Kinetic Energy ($KE$) at the bottom of the track.
    $$PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$$

2.  **Stage 2: The Collision (Conservation of Linear Momentum)**
    When the moving block hits the stationary block and they stick together, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**. Mechanical energy is *not* conserved during this collision (some is lost to sound, heat, and deformation), but the total linear momentum ($p$) of the isolated system is conserved.
    $$p_{initial} = p_{final}$$



---

### Step 1: Determine the Speed of the First Block at the Bottom

**1. Define the energy equation for the slide:**
The potential energy at the top is $m_1gh$. The kinetic energy at the bottom is $\frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$.
$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

**2. Isolate the velocity ($v_1$):**
Notice that the mass ($m_1$) appears on both sides of the equation, so it cancels out. This means the speed at the bottom depends only on the height and gravity, not the mass of the sliding block.
$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v_1^2$$
$$v_1^2 = 2gh$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$

**3. Substitute the known values and calculate:**
* Height ($h$) = **3.0 m**
* Gravity ($g$) $\approx$ **9.8 m/s²**

$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}$$
$$v_1 \approx 7.668 \text{ m/s}$$

The first block is traveling at approximately 7.668 m/s right before the collision.

---

### Step 2: Determine the Final Speed of the Combined Mass



[Image of perfectly inelastic collision]


**1. Define the momentum equation for the collision:**
Before the collision, only the first block is moving. After the collision, both blocks move together as a single combined mass ($m_1 + m_2$) with a final velocity ($v_f$).
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Since the second block is initially at rest, its initial velocity ($v_2$) is 0, making its initial momentum zero.
$$m_1v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

**2. Isolate the final velocity ($v_f$):**
$$v_f = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1 + m_2}$$

**3. Substitute the known values and calculate:**
* Mass 1 ($m_1$) = **0.5 kg**
* Initial speed of Mass 1 ($v_1$) = **7.668 m/s**
* Mass 2 ($m_2$) = **1.5 kg**

$$v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.668}{0.5 + 1.5}$$
$$v_f = \frac{3.834}{2.0}$$
$$v_f = 1.917 \text{ m/s}$$

**Answer:** The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is exactly **1.917 m/s**.
