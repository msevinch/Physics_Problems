### 9. Momentum Comparison

### Fundamental Theory

**Momentum** ($p$) is a fundamental concept in physics that describes the quantity of motion an object has. It depends on two variables: how much "stuff" is moving (mass) and how fast that "stuff" is moving (velocity). Because it takes both mass and velocity into account, a small object moving very fast can have the same momentum as a large object moving very slowly.

The formula for calculating linear momentum is:
$$p = m \cdot v$$
*(where $p$ is momentum, $m$ is mass, and $v$ is velocity).*

To get the standard SI unit for momentum—kilogram-meters per second ($\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$)—we must ensure our mass is in kilograms and our velocity is in meters per second.



---

### Step 1: Calculate the Momentum of the Fly

**1. Identify knowns and convert units:**
* Mass of fly ($m_{fly}$) = $2 \text{ grams}$
To convert grams to kilograms, divide by 1000:
$$m_{fly} = \frac{2}{1000} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$$
* Velocity of fly ($v_{fly}$) = $10 \text{ m/s}$

**2. Apply the formula:**
$$p_{fly} = m_{fly} \cdot v_{fly}$$
$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \text{ kg} \cdot 10 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_{fly} = 0.020 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate the Momentum of the Tennis Ball

**1. Identify knowns and convert units:**
* Mass of ball ($m_{ball}$) = $60 \text{ grams}$
To convert grams to kilograms, divide by 1000:
$$m_{ball} = \frac{60}{1000} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$$
* Velocity of ball ($v_{ball}$) = $1 \text{ m/s}$

**2. Apply the formula:**
$$p_{ball} = m_{ball} \cdot v_{ball}$$
$$p_{ball} = 0.060 \text{ kg} \cdot 1 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_{ball} = 0.060 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### Step 3: Compare and Determine

Now we simply compare the two final values:
* Fly's momentum: **$0.020 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$**
* Tennis ball's momentum: **$0.060 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$**

$$0.060 > 0.020$$

**Answer:** The **tennis ball** has greater momentum. Even though the fly is moving ten times faster, the tennis ball is thirty times heavier. The massive difference in weight easily overcomes the fly's speed advantage.
